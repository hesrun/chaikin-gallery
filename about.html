ARTIST

Konstantin Chaykin is a Russian watchmaker, founder of his own manufactory, a member of the Académie Horlogère des Créateurs Indépendant, and winner of multiple international awards, including the prestigious Grand Prix d'Horlogerie de Genève (GPHG).

Chaykin thinks of himself not only as a watchmaker and inventor, but also as an artist. Traveling the globe, he always takes time to visit museums, and he's inspired both by the legacy of ages past and contemporary art.

Konstantin Chaykin believes that for a watchmaker art is not just a source of new ideas, but a "raw material" that should be "passed through one's imagination," reinterpreted, and seen in a new light.

The history of human culture is the history of interpretation, reinvention and reimagining, and watchmaking is no exception.

The work of a watchmaker is also partly art. When thinking over and creating his projects, Chaykin composes many pictures, from quick sketches in his moleskine notebook to complete watercolor drawings. Not all of them turn into watches: "Any creative person has a collection of unfinished projects, 'untold stories.' And I think it's beautiful," says Konstantin Chaykin. Indeed, a folder full of sketches of still-imaginary watches serves as a great creative incentive, which may, in time, push the master towards new ideas and novel creations.

Classic paintings and sculptures, plots from literary masterpieces and religious narratives, movies and comics, abstract paintings, and videos – everything becomes "raw material" for the art of watchmaking. Myths of Chronos or the Minotaur's labyrinth, Hoffmann's and Carroll's fairy tales, vampire legends and commedia dell'arte performances, the Mexican holiday Day of the Dead, and many other stories evoke vibrant images in the artist's imagination. Committing them to paper with his sketches, Chaykin created his own graphics gallery. It's not limited to technical drawings for future watch collections and reveals the watchmaking master's artistic side.
